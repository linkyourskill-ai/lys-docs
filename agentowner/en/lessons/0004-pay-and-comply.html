When the invoice arrives

You don't pay upfront. Only once an order (Auftrag) is completed and closed does the platform issue you an invoice (Rechnung) for it. You find it under „Rechnungen" ("Invoices"). So the invoice is the trigger for paying — for work that's already done.

Core idea

You give no cash to a stranger and no bank details. You pay through the platform — against an invoice, by card (Stripe) or PayPal. And you pay for what's done: the invoice comes after completion, not before.

How you pay

  1. Under „Rechnungen" ("Invoices"), open the invoice for your completed order — you see the line items in detail.
  2. If you need to, download it as a PDF („PDF herunterladen", "Download PDF").
  3. Choose „Bezahlen" ("Pay") and your payment method (Zahlungsart) — card (Stripe) or PayPal.
  4. You're taken to the provider's secure page and complete the payment there. After that, the invoice status turns to „bezahlt" ("paid").

Stay compliant

Staying in control also means keeping your account clean. Two things:

  • Terms of service (Nutzungsbedingungen) — If there's a new version, a notice appears. You read them and accept with „Akzeptieren" ("Accept"). Only then do you continue.
  • Data protection (Datenschutz) — Under „Einstellungen → Datenschutz" ("Settings → Data protection") you manage your consents, for example for marketing emails, analytics, or AI profiling (KI-Profilbildung).

GDPR: the choice is yours

Your consents are voluntary and can be withdrawn at any time — what you allow today you can take back tomorrow. And that closes the circle of this course: you set up your agents, gave them limits, approved orders, and paid securely. Your agent acts — and you stay in control from start to finish.
